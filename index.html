Get to know Canada

Canada is a vast and diverse country, known for its stunning natural landscapes, friendly people, and multicultural society. From the towering Rocky Mountains to the rugged coastlines of the Atlantic, Canada offers a wealth of outdoor adventures and breathtaking scenery. Its major cities like Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal are vibrant hubs of culture, cuisine, and innovation. With a rich history shaped by Indigenous peoples, French and British colonization, and immigration from around the world, Canada boasts a unique identity and a welcoming atmosphere.

Learn more about Canada's provinces and territories

Canada is divided into ten provinces and three territories, each offering a unique blend of geography, culture, and history. The provinces, from east to west, are Newfoundland and Labrador, Prince Edward Island, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Quebec, Ontario, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Alberta, and British Columbia. Each province has its own distinct character, from the maritime charm of the Atlantic provinces to the cosmopolitan cities of Ontario and the rugged landscapes of the western provinces. The territories – Yukon, Northwest Territories, and Nunavut – are located in the northern part of Canada and are known for their vast wilderness, Indigenous cultures, and unique Arctic environments. Exploring Canada's provinces and territories provides a fascinating journey through diverse landscapes and vibrant communities.

Alberta public holidays

Alberta observes several public holidays throughout the year, providing residents with opportunities to celebrate cultural events and enjoy time with family and friends. Key holidays include New Year’s Day on January 1st, which marks the beginning of the year, and Family Day, celebrated on the third Monday in February, dedicated to spending quality time with loved ones. In May, Victoria Day honors Queen Victoria’s birthday, while Canada Day on July 1st commemorates the country’s confederation. Labour Day, observed on the first Monday in September, recognizes the contributions of workers, and Thanksgiving is celebrated on the second Monday in October, allowing people to reflect on their blessings. Finally, Christmas Day on December 25th and Boxing Day on December 26th round out the year, bringing festive cheer and family gatherings.

National and Provincial Parks in Alberta

Alberta is home to a stunning array of national and provincial parks, each offering unique landscapes and recreational opportunities. Banff National Park, perhaps the most famous, boasts majestic mountains, turquoise lakes, and abundant wildlife. Jasper National Park, equally impressive, features vast wilderness areas and the renowned Columbia Icefield. In addition to these national treasures, Alberta's provincial parks, such as Kananaskis Country and Dinosaur Provincial Park, provide diverse experiences ranging from hiking and camping to exploring ancient fossil beds. These protected areas are vital for preserving Alberta's natural heritage and providing spaces for outdoor enthusiasts to connect with nature.
